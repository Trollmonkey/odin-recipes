Creamy Mac & Cheese

To the best of my memory, this is the very first recipe my mother taught me when I was a wee lad. Now, I've added just a couple of Ingredients to really make the flavours pop. Ooey, gooey, creamy mac & cheese!

Ingredients

  • 2c Macaroni Noodles
  • 1c Milk (I prefer whole milk, but any will do)
  • 1c Grated Cheese (I like a mix of 75:25 aged cheddar:smoked gouda)
  • 2tbsp Butter
  • 2tbsp Flour
  • 1tsp Dijon or Grain mustard
  • 1tsp Fresh Grated Nutmeg
  • 1tsp Garlic Powder

Steps

  1. Get some water on the stove! Even if you need more time, there's nothing wrong with having your water boiling and ready to cook your pasta.
  2. In a medium saucepan, melt your butter and wait for it to foam up a little bit.
  3. Once your butter is foaming, whisk in your flour until fully incorporated.
  4. Once your roux (the flour and butter mixture you just made) is formed, slowly whisk in your milk.
  5. This is a good time to toss your pasta in the boiling water and set your timer according to the package's instructions. Remember to salt your water before adding your pasta. It ought to be salty!
  6. Start slowly adding your cheese to your roux. Once incorporated, turn down the heat and add your mustard, nutmeg, and garlic powder.
  7. Drain your pasta once it's al dente (that means there's a bit of a bite left to the pasta, but it's fully cooked). Reserve 1tbsp of the pasta water.
  8. Dump the sauce unceremoniously into the pasta, add the pasta water you reserved, and mix it all together.
  9. Enjoy!
